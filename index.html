About Me

I am a Frontend Web Developer at the National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) in Golden, Colorado, where I serve as the primary coder for various NREL.gov subsites, intranet sites, and U.S. Department of Energy websites supporting renewable energy research. Previously, I was a Web Editor for Leidos, supporting the National Science Foundation's U.S. Antarctic Program (USAP), including a notable deployment to the South Pole to launch a new intranet for station personnel.

I work with HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap, and Processing, with experience in object-oriented programming, computational media, and data visualization. I combine these coding skills with UI/UX design capabilities and technical writing expertise, working across Visual Studio Code, Adobe Creative Cloud, Git, email marketing platforms, and content management systems. I also serve as a technical resource for email and list management tool selection.

My interdisciplinary education—M.A. in Art and Technology, Full Stack Development certificate, B.S. in English – Professional Writing with an HCI/UX minor, and A.S. in Business Administration – Paralegal Studies—has shown me that innovative solutions emerge at the intersections between fields. I approach frontend development by integrating creative expression, technical precision, user-centered thinking, and research methodology to create digital experiences that serve both technical requirements and the people who use them.
